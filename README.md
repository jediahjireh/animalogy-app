# Animalogy

Learn Safety Through Animal Stories — a gamified educational tool teaching children about safety, trust, and digital awareness through culturally-relevant animal analogies.

## Author

**Built by [Jediah Jireh Naicker](https://github.com/jediahjireh)**

## Overview

Animalogy uses real animal behaviours from five world regions to help children understand complex social situations — online deception, false alarms, flattery, hidden danger, and betrayal of trust — in a way that feels familiar and engaging.

### Story Packs

| Story                               | Mascot               | Region          | Safety Theme            |
| ----------------------------------- | -------------------- | --------------- | ----------------------- |
| Kaia and the Bird Who Cried Danger  | Kaia (Meerkat)       | Southern Africa | Online Deception        |
| Daisy and the Laughing Trickster    | Daisy (Dingo)        | Australia       | False Alarms            |
| Tara and the Crocodile's Compliment | Tara (Star Tortoise) | India           | Flattery & Manipulation |
| Luma and the Sneaky Snake           | Luma (Capybara)      | Brazil          | Hidden Danger           |
| Ricky and the Owl That Lied         | Ricky (Raccoon)      | North America   | Betrayal of Trust       |

Each pack includes 8 illustrated story pages, an educator guide with learning objectives, discussion prompts, and hands-on activities across three age groups (4–6, 7–9, 10–12).

### Modes

- **Child Mode**: Larger text, simplified navigation, story-focused experience
- **Educator Mode**: Full access to guides, discussion prompts, activities, and pedagogical framing

## Tech Stack

- **Flutter** (Dart)
- **Riverpod** for state management
- **GoRouter** for declarative routing
- **SharedPreferences** for local persistence
- **Google Fonts** (Nunito) for typography

100% offline, no auth, no payments, no external APIs. All content is pre-seeded at compile time.
